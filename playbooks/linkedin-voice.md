# LinkedIn Voice — Tim Eun (Warm Follow-Up)

LinkedIn messages in this workflow are **not cold outreach.** The contact has already:
1. Received a cold email from Raleigh
2. Opened it (confirmed by HubSpot tracking)
3. Not yet replied or booked

The LinkedIn message is Tim (CEO) reaching out personally. This is a natural escalation — the company already reached out, now the founder is getting in touch to get the demo on the calendar.

**Read [email-voice.md](email-voice.md) first.** Core voice rules (no exclamation points in sales, no hollow openers, no em-dashes, no corporate buzzwords) still apply. This file covers what's *different* for LinkedIn.

---

## What must be true before drafting

Before writing any message, gather:

1. **Original email subject line / deal hook** — for continuity. The contact saw this in their inbox; referencing the same angle proves this isn't spray-and-pray.
2. **Company name** — appears in every message.
3. **Contact title** — calibrates tone (MD gets more direct; Associate slightly warmer).

Pull original email context from HubSpot engagement history (`hs_email_direction: FORWARDED_EMAIL`). If not available, fall back to the task body. If neither, run abbreviated research ([research.md](research.md)).

---

## Framing

- **Tim introduces himself as CEO — flowing directly into what Socratics does.** One motion, not a stiff intro followed by a separate pitch. "I'm Tim, CEO at Socratics. We build..." is correct. "I'm Tim Eun, CEO of Socratics.ai. I wanted to reach out..." is wrong.
- **Acknowledge prior contact when natural**, never reference tracking: "my team sent over some info on..." works; "I saw you opened my email" does not.
- **Assumptive demo ask.** Ask *when*, not *if*. 15 minutes.
- **Reuse the original email's deal hook.** Rephrase it for LinkedIn — don't paste the subject line.
- **CEO weight matters.** Prospect got an email from GTM; now the founder is reaching out personally. This signals they're prioritized.

## Formula (connection request)

1. Name + Tim intro flowing into what Socratics does (1–2 sentences)
2. Connect to personalization token — deal name or firm-specific hook (1 sentence)
3. Assumptive demo ask (1 sentence)

---

## Formats

### Connection request (300 char hard limit, <250 preferred)

The primary format. Tim is connecting with someone who got Raleigh's email.

**Rules:**
- MUST be under 300 characters
- No greeting line — name inline
- No sign-off, no links
- Company name appears once
- Same deal/personalization token as the email
- Do NOT say "I saw you opened my email"

**Examples:**

*With deal attribution (strong):*
```
Derek, I'm Tim, CEO at Socratics. We build audit-ready financial models in under 10 minutes for PE teams. Curious how the ABH model buildout went at Clearview. When do you have 15 minutes for a demo?
```
(200 chars)

*Firm-level fallback:*
```
Michael, I run Socratics.ai, we're building institutional financial models in under 10 minutes specifically for IB teams. Would love to show the iMerge team how it works. When works for a quick demo?
```
(199 chars)

*Senior-level (MD/Partner):*
```
Harry, I'm Tim, I run Socratics.ai. We build audit-ready 3-statement models in under 10 minutes for teams like Lempriere Wells. Worth a quick demo?
```
(147 chars)

### Direct message (600 char soft limit, under 400 preferred)

Used when already connected.

**Structure:**
1. Name + Tim intro flowing into Socratics (1–2 sentences)
2. Deal hook + value prop detail (1–2 sentences)
3. Assumptive demo ask with company name (1 sentence)

**Example:**
```
Derek, I'm Tim, CEO at Socratics.ai. We build institutional-grade 3-statement models in under 10 minutes, specifically for PE teams. Formula-linked, fully traceable, every assumption visible.

If the ABH model build was anything like what we typically see, this would save the Clearview team a lot of hours. 200+ PE teams and banks use us to skip the rebuild and go straight to analysis.

When do you have 15 minutes for a quick demo?
```
(435 chars)

### Re-engagement (400 char soft limit)

For contacts who opened email a while ago but never engaged further. LinkedIn task may be a 2nd or 3rd attempt.

**Rules:**
- Do NOT guilt-trip or reference touch count
- Assume busy, not disinterested
- Softer CTA: "if the timing works" or "happy to set something up when it makes sense"
- Company name once

**Example:**
```
Jas, I'm Tim, CEO at Socratics.ai. My team reached out a while back about financial modeling for the Lark Street team. If model builds are ever eating up more time than they should, happy to show you what we've built. No rush.
```
(226 chars)

## LinkedIn vs. email differences

| Dimension | Email (cold) | LinkedIn (warm follow-up) |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------------|
| Context | First touch | They opened the email, touch #2+ |
| Sender | Raleigh | Tim |
| Framing | "Here's what we do" | "Our team sent something over, wanted to connect personally" |
| Subject line | Required, deal-hook-driven | None (connection) or optional (InMail) |
| Greeting | "Hi [Name]," on own line | Name inline, no "Hi" |
| Sign-off | `Best,` or none | None |
| Length | 4–6 sentences | 2–3 (connection) or 3–5 (DM) |
| Links | None on first touch | None |
| Explain Socratics | Yes, they don't know you | Brief — they've seen the email |

## Character limits (LinkedIn-enforced)

| Type | Hard limit | Target |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Connection request | 300 | <250 |
| InMail subject | 200 | <60 (match email style) |
| InMail body | 1900 | <600 |
| Regular DM | 8000 | <400 |

Always count characters and report them.

## InMail subject lines

Rephrase the email subject, don't copy it. Keep under 60 chars. Vary structure.

- Email was `Financial models for the ABH deal` → InMail: `ABH modeling, quick demo`
- Email was `Faster modeling for Oaklins` → InMail: `Oaklins deal team, 10-minute models`

---

## What NOT to do

| ❌ Don't | ✓ Do |
|---------|------|
| "I'm Tim Eun, CEO of Socratics.ai. I wanted to reach out..." | "I'm Tim, CEO at Socratics. We build..." (intro flows into pitch) |
| Stiff intro + separate pitch | One motion — who I am + what we do in a single breath |
| "I saw you opened our email" | Reference the topic, not the tracking |
| "Just following up on Raleigh's email" | "My team sent over some info on..." or lead with the hook |
| Repeat the email verbatim | Rephrase the same hook in shorter form |
| "Would you be open to a call?" | "When do you have 15 minutes for a demo?" |
| Blank connection request | Always include a message |
| Calendar links | Ask for their availability |
| Hashtags or emojis | Plain text only |
| Mention LinkedIn itself | Reference their work, not the platform |
