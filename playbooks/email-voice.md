# Email Voice — Raleigh Dexel

Write emails indistinguishable from Raleigh's natural voice.

See [company/positioning.md](../company/positioning.md) for what Socratics does and the core value prop. This file is about **how to say it.**

---

## Core voice

- **Tone:** Direct, calm, confident. Never pushy or salesy. Conversational but professional.
- **Energy:** Low-key but sharp. Lets the insight do the work. Doesn't oversell.
- **Length:** Short. Every sentence earns its place. No fluff, no filler.
- **Formality:** Casual-professional. Not corporate. Not bro-y.

---

## Non-negotiables

1. **Short paragraphs.** 1–3 sentences max. White space is intentional.
2. **No exclamation points** in sales / outreach. Rare in personal (only genuine enthusiasm).
3. **No name in sign-off.** Raleigh's signature handles the rest. Email ends at `Best,`.
4. **Sign-off:** `Best,` — always. Never "Best regards," "Thanks," "Cheers," or "Sincerely."
5. **Cold outreach (first touch): no sign-off at all.** Signature handles it.
6. **No em-dashes.** Commas or new sentences instead.
7. **No hollow openers.** Never "Hope you're doing well," "I wanted to reach out," "Just following up."
8. **One ask per email.** Never stack questions or CTAs.
9. **Typos are human.** Don't over-polish. Don't intentionally introduce them, don't flag them either.
10. **No corporate buzzwords.** No "synergy," "circle back" (unless echoing), "touch base," "leverage," "value proposition."
11. **Product name casing:** `socratics.ai` in flowing text; `Socratics.ai` when proper noun (start of sentence or subject line).

## Cold outreach structure

### Subject line

MUST contain:
1. A personalization token (priority: specific deal > firm name as fallback).
2. Clear reference to **financial modeling** (so the pitch is obvious from the subject).

Keep under 60 characters. No punctuation at the end. Vary structure every time — don't repeat patterns.

**Examples (each structured differently):**
- `Financial models for the 360insights deal`
- `Quicker financial modeling for ABH`
- `10-minute models on the UpSwell add-ons`
- `Modeling Key Container in under 10 minutes`
- `Financial models for Revo Brands`
- `Faster modeling on the Barcode Group deal`
- `Accelerant IPO: institutional models, 10 minutes`
- `Financial models for the Clearview portfolio` (firm fallback)
- `Faster modeling for the Hennepin team` (firm fallback)

### Opener

**Must lead with product value tied to their specific deal.** The reader decides in this sentence whether to keep reading — front-load the value.

**Strong opener patterns:**
- "What if the 3-statement model for the [Deal] acquisition was done in 10 minutes instead of 10 hours?"
- "Raw financials to a fully linked 3-statement model in 10 minutes. That's what we're building at Socratics.ai."
- "[Deal type] financials are some of the messiest to model from scratch. What if the [Deal] 3-statement model was done in 10 minutes?"
- "What if every 3-statement model at [Company] was built in 10 minutes, fully formula-linked and audit-ready?"
- "A fully linked 3-statement model for the [Deal] sale, built in 10 minutes from raw financials. That's what we're building at Socratics.ai."

**CRITICAL: Always pair the deal name with a descriptor.** Bare names mean nothing.
- "the Spartaco acquisition" / "the Spartaco platform investment"
- "the Franchise Creator sale" / "the Franchise Creator mandate"
- "the Doctors of PT deal" / "the Doctors of PT acquisition"
- "the RF Technologies investment" / "the ABH recapitalization"
- "the Interwest Safety sell-side" / "the FORTA transaction"

Descriptors: acquisition, sale, investment, recapitalization, mandate, platform investment, exit, add-on, close, deal (safe fallback).

**Never open with:**
- "Curious how..." / "How long did..." / "How much time does your team spend..."
- "I wanted to reach out..." / "Hope you're doing well..."
- Bare deal names with no context

### Body

1–2 short paragraphs max. **Must include "At Socratics.ai, we..."** — names the company and flows into the value prop.

Write like a human explaining to a peer, not a feature list. Hit the pain point (upstream time loss, messy data, manual rebuilds, numbers that don't tie out) and connect it to what Socratics fixes.

**Good body patterns:**
- "At Socratics.ai, we take raw financials and turn them into audit-ready, formula-linked models. No rebuilding from scratch, no chasing numbers that don't tie out."
- "At Socratics.ai, we handle the piece that eats up the most time, structuring raw financials into clean 3-statement models. Every assumption traceable, no numbers that don't tie out."
- "At Socratics.ai, we handle the entire intake layer. Raw data goes in, audit-ready, formula-linked models come out in under 10 minutes."

The "200+ PE teams and banks use us" proof point fits when space allows. Total email 4–6 sentences.

### CTA

**Assumptive.** Assume the meeting is happening and ask for availability. Always mention 15 minutes. Do NOT suggest specific days. Do NOT ask *if* — ask *when*. **MUST name the company team.**

- "What does your schedule look like for 15 minutes? Happy to show the [Company] team how it works."
- "When works for a quick 15-minute call? Would love to show the [Company] team what this looks like."
- "What does your schedule look like for 15 minutes? Happy to walk the [Company] team through it."
- "Would love to show the [Company] team what this looks like. When works for 15 minutes?"

### Sign-off

**None.** No "Best," on cold touch #1. Signature handles it.

### Personalization scope

- DO reference: company name, sector/industry focus, recent deal.
- DO NOT reference: career history, previous firms, education, LinkedIn posts, personal background, time at firm.
- Personalization should feel like you know their work, not like you stalked their profile.

## Follow-up (prospect went cold / bad timing)

- Acknowledge briefly, don't guilt-trip.
- Offer value without demanding attention.
- Leave the door open without being needy.
- 3–5 sentences total.

**Example:**
```
Hi Lisa,

No worries, I'll circle back in a couple of months. In the meantime, if helpful, I can send over a short overview and a couple examples so you have a sense of how teams are using Socratics.ai.

If anything opens up sooner, feel free to reach out.

Best,
```

## Objection / rejection reply

- No arguing, no re-pitching aggressively.
- Acknowledge their reasoning as valid.
- Plant a seed for the future without pressure.
- End graciously — never burn a bridge.

**Example (ROI pushback):**
```
Hi Colin,

Appreciate the candid feedback.

Totally fair at your current scale. Where we've seen it start to matter more is when deal volume picks up or when consistency across analysts becomes a bigger factor in underwriting quality, not just speed.

We'll stay out of your way for now. But if anything changes, happy to reconnect.

Thanks again for taking the time.

Best,
```

**Example (new stack / not the right time):**
```
Hey Marcus,

Understood, we'll step back for now.

Once you get that new stack implemented, let me know if theres a gap we can fill, especially around structuring financials upstream or tightening model consistency. Happy to reconnect.

Best,
```
(Note "Hey" for warmer contacts already on informal terms.)

## Warm inbound / referral

- Thank briefly, don't over-thank.
- Connect their problem to what Socratics.ai does immediately.
- Offer a specific next step (times + calendar link).

**Example:**
```
Derek,

Appreciate you reaching out and glad Tom connected us.

That's exactly the problem we're focused on. Most of the variance and time loss we see happens before modeling even starts, just getting financials into a clean, consistent structure.

Happy to walk you through our process. Im open [these times of the week] or you can book some time on my calendar here.

Best,
```

## Networking / personal

- Warmer but still tight.
- Genuine curiosity about what they're building.
- Reciprocal — share 1–2 sentences on current work.
- Ends with a light, open availability ask.

**Example:**
```
Jordan,

Good to hear from you!

At socratics.ai, we've been working on building infrastructure that takes raw financials and turns them into structured, audit-ready models. The focus is really on fixing the intake layer so teams aren't dealing with inconsistencies downstream in underwriting / modeling.

Would be great to catch up. What does your schedule look like over the next week or so?
```

---

## Intro / double-opt-in

- Acknowledge the connector inline.
- One-sentence what-he-does summary.
- Genuine curiosity about the other person first.
- Soft ask for a call with availability.

**Example:**
```
Hi Alex,

Great to meet you, and appreciate the intro Sam

I'm working on Socratics.ai, focused on structuring raw financial data into clean, audit-ready models so teams can move faster without introducing inconsistency upstream.

Would be great to hear more about what you're building in fintech infra. Always interesting to compare notes in this space.

What does your availability look like for a call next week?

Best,
```

## Speaker / opportunity request

- Genuine enthusiasm (one `!` acceptable).
- Connect topic to current work naturally.
- Ask for details before committing — practical, not evasive.

**Example:**
```
Priay,

Appreciate you thinking of me! sounds like a great opportunity for us. The lower middle market is exactly where we're seeing a lot of the friction around financial data and modeling.

Could you share a bit more detail on the format (panel size, topics, timing, location)? Happy to make it work if it lines up.

Best,
```

## What NOT to do

| ❌ Don't | ✓ Do instead |
|---------|-------------|
| "Hope this finds you well!" | Jump straight in |
| "I wanted to reach out because..." | State the point directly |
| "Just following up on my last email" | Reference context naturally or skip it |
| "Please let me know if you have any questions" | End with a specific ask |
| "We'd love to partner with you" | "Happy to walk you through our process" |
| Long bullet-point feature lists | 1–2 sentences on the core insight |
| Exclamation points in cold / sales | Period. Period. Period. |
| "Best regards," | "Best," |
| Writing a name after sign-off | Stop at `Best,` |
| Restating everything in a follow-up | Assume they remember |
| Attachments or images in cold outreach | Offer to send materials only if asked |
| "Checking in" / "Quick question" subjects | Specific hooks tied to their situation |
| Multiple CTAs or questions | One ask, one path forward |

## Pre-send checklist

- [ ] Subject under 50 characters and specific
- [ ] Opens with a hook, not a hollow greeting
- [ ] Body leads with their problem, not Socratics features
- [ ] Under 150 words for follow-ups and replies
- [ ] Single CTA — low friction, not pushy
- [ ] No bullet lists in emails under 200 words
- [ ] No exclamation points in sales context
- [ ] Sign-off correct (`Best,` or none for cold touch #1)
- [ ] No links or attachments in first-touch cold emails
- [ ] Sounds like a human, not a template
