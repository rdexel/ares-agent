# Current Quarter — Q2 2026

## The motion

**Lock down 6–10 reference customers.** INSPIRED-definition: customers who pay, use the product regularly, and talk to us frequently. These are the flywheel — they de-risk adoption for everyone after them and give us the product signal we need to build toward v2.

## Priorities

1. **Get prospects onto the platform.** v1 (alpha) is in market. It's okay, not great. The bottleneck right now isn't interest — it's getting people through the door to actually try it on their own data. Every demo should end with them onboarded, not "we'll circle back."
2. **Close reference deals.** Pay + use + talk. Not free pilots. Not one-time demos. A reference customer has a contract, is in the platform weekly, and will take a call from a prospect.
3. **Feed v2 with real user signal.** v2 expands the surface area — Socratics Compose (multi-doc consolidation) and Model (full suite). We have functioning prototypes users can test with their own data. Every reference customer should be a design partner candidate for v2 features.

## Target

- **6–10 reference customers by end of quarter.** Pay, use, talk.
- Each one should be someone Raleigh and Tim could call tomorrow and get a real-time read.

## Strategic pushes

- **Push into trials aggressively.** If a prospect says "interesting," the next step is access, not another meeting. Overdue access links (e.g., Class VI) are the enemy of this quarter — kill that lag.
- **Design partnerships where the fit is clear.** Turtleback / oil & gas is the canonical example. A design partnership is a path to reference-customer status that compounds product + relationship at once.
- **v1 → v2 bridge.** When a v1 user hits a limit, the answer is "try this v2 prototype," not "wait until it's released." Use the gap as a conversion event.

## Known constraints

- **v1 is okay, not great.** We can't pitch it as finished. Sell the trajectory — what it is *becoming* via v2 — and get them in now so they shape it.
- **Self-serve onboarding is the weak link.** Until it improves, onboarding is high-touch. Plan capacity accordingly — Raleigh/Tim have to be in the loop for most first-trial sessions.

## How this shapes campaigns

- Cold outreach (Analyst / Reviewer / IC Buyer sequences) should favor firms where a reference relationship is plausible — not broadest reach. Quality > volume.
- LinkedIn warm follow-up (Tim) is the CEO-weight escalation that gets high-potential prospects across the trial-access line.
- Daily report's "going cold" section matters more than usual this quarter — every stalled trial is a lost reference candidate.
